CARNIVAL IN ST. CROIX

Growing up on the tropical island of St. Croix, U.S.V.I., I was always immersed with various cultural experinces. For instance, every year, there is a big party called Carnival. Carnival is an island-wide event that celebrates the unqiue culture of the island. Everyone spends months preparing for the event as it brings in the most tourism revenue of any other event on the island.

There are many different ways for the people of the island to particapte in Carnival. Many fisherman showcase unique dishes with their fresh catch, and many women make delicous pastries out of local fruits such as guava and mango. Local artists create jewelry with shells and seaglass. School bands plan their marches, and the famous carnival parade dancers spend months designing and sewing elaborate costumes with feathers and jewels. However, the mocko jumbies bring everything together. Men, women and children perch high up on stilts and dress in bright colors and masks.

As a partipant in carvial, I got to experince all the splendor of being high up on stilts. I practiced for weeks, walking all around my house six feet above the ground, trying my best to balance. Finally, the big night came, and once my costume was on, I felt invicible. I danced all night long, and seeing the joy on childrens faces as they looked up at me in awe is a feeling I will never forget. I liked LOVED being a part of something bigger than myself and so cultually rich.
